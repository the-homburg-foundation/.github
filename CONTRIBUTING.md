# Homburg Contribution Guidelines  
_Last updated: 11/26/25_

*Participating in The Homburg Foundation projects assumes you have read and agreed to the Homburg Contribution Guidelines.*

---

## 1. Purpose
The Homburg Foundation develops and maintains free and open software. These guidelines define expected standards for contributions. Anyone may contribute or maintain, but governance and sponsorship rights are reserved for Foundation members.

## 2. Contribution Standards
Contributors must:
- Act professionally and respectfully.  
- Provide clear, well-documented, and technically sound contributions.  
- Follow project-specific coding and documentation standards.  
- Collaborate in good faith and avoid disruptive behavior.  

## 3. Scope
These guidelines apply to all contributions to Foundation-managed repositories, including code, documentation, and other content.

## 4. Governance and Sponsorship
Only Foundation members may approve, sponsor, or govern contributions. Members must:
- Exercise duties impartially and transparently.  
- Enforce guidelines consistently.  
- Demonstrate exemplary conduct in all reviews and merges.

## 5. Reporting Issues
Report problems or violations of these guidelines through the project's defined reporting channels. Include relevant context and evidence. Reports are handled confidentially.

## 6. Enforcement
Actions for violations may include:
- Warnings.  
- Temporary or permanent restrictions on contribution access.  
- Removal from project activities.  
- For members: suspension or revocation of governance privileges.

Enforcement is determined by the severity and impact of the violation.

## 7. Amendments
The Foundation may update these guidelines as needed. All revisions are dated and versioned.

---

By contributing to The Homburg Foundation, you agree to comply with these Contribution Guidelines.
